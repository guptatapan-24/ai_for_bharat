VerdictIQ - Full lifecycle court judgment intelligence system for government legal compliance
Description
*
WE ARE BUILDING: VerdictIQ is a full lifecycle court judgment intelligence system for government legal compliance - from the moment a judgment PDF is fetched from the High Court's CIS integration to the moment a verified, department-ready action plan appears on a decision-maker's dashboard. It replaces the entire manual reading and interpretation process, not just one step of it. A judgment lands in CCMS via API. The system reads it, extracts every material directive automatically, classifies each one by type and urgency, and builds a structured action plan. The action plan - with source highlights, confidence scores, and extracted timelines - is then placed in front of a human reviewer who approves, edits, or rejects every field before it moves forward. Nothing reaches the dashboard unverified. Every decision is logged. It is a judgment intelligence engine using document understanding, legal NLP, and a mandatory human-in-the-loop architecture. It reads every page, applies every directive, produces an action item with a source citation for every single decision, and refuses to let any critical order go unnoticed.
The system works in four steps. 1. Judgment Understanding - The system ingests the judgment PDF - scanned or digital, single-page or hundred-page - and extracts every material element into a structured record. Case number, court, bench, date of order, parties involved, and case history are pulled first. Every directive, compliance obligation, and judicial observation is then identified, classified by type - compliance order, stay, direction to a specific department, limitation period trigger, appeal consideration - and annotated with its explicit or inferred deadline, the responsible authority, and the nature of action required. It structurally parses legal language with jurisdictional classification. 2. Action Plan Generation - Based on extracted directives, the system constructs a department-ready action plan. Each item in the plan specifies what is required: compliance with a specific order, consideration of an appeal within the limitation window, coordination between departments, or escalation to a senior authority. Timelines are populated from explicit dates in the judgment where available, and inferred from standard legal limitation periods where not. Responsible departments are identified from the case parties and the nature of the directive. Every action item is flagged as mandatory or advisory using the judgment's own language, not a guess. Novel or ambiguous directives are clearly flagged as requiring expert legal review before any administrative decision is taken. 3. Human Verification - No extracted data and no action plan item moves forward without human sign-off. The verification interface presents every extracted field alongside its exact source highlight in the original PDF - the reviewer sees not just the extracted value but the precise sentence it came from, the page number, and the confidence score the model assigned. The reviewer can approve the field as extracted, edit it with a corrected value and a stated reason, or reject it entirely. Only fully verified records - where every field has been explicitly approved or corrected by a human reviewer - are passed to the dashboard. Nothing is silently promoted. 4. Dashboard and Compliance View - The dashboard displays only verified action plans. The view is structured by department, showing each department's pending actions, upcoming deadlines, nature of compliance required, and case reference. Overdue items are surfaced prominently. Decision-makers see clean, reliable, human-endorsed information - not raw AI output, not probabilistic extractions, not unreviewed text. The complete audit trail from PDF ingestion through extraction, action plan generation, human review decisions, and dashboard display is logged and accessible.
The four non-negotiables. Every action item has a source: not "compliance required." The reviewer sees the exact directive text, the page number, the extracted deadline, the identified responsible department, and why the system classified it as mandatory or advisory. That is what a legally defensible action record means in a government compliance context. Mandatory and advisory directives are a legal distinction: acting on an advisory as if it were mandatory, or missing a mandatory order because it was buried in obiter, are both consequential errors. The system classifies these from the judgment's own language. Nothing reaches the dashboard unverified: the human-in-the-loop is not an optional review step appended at the end - it is the architectural gate between AI output and administrative action. No field, no timeline, no department assignment is displayed to a decision-maker unless a human reviewer has explicitly approved or corrected it. The audit trail must survive administrative and legal scrutiny: every extraction logs model version, source PDF hash, extracted value, confidence score, and the reviewer's decision. Every edit records the original extracted value alongside the reviewer's correction and stated reason.
Domain depth that makes the difference. The gap between a demo and a system a Centre for e-Governance deployment officer actually uses is entirely in the edge cases. VerdictIQ handles: scanned judgment OCR with confidence scoring at the page level so low-quality scans are flagged rather than silently misread, multi-directional orders where a single judgment issues directives to three different departments with different timelines, inferred limitation period calculation where the judgment does not state an appeal deadline explicitly but the applicable statute determines one, distinction between operative directions and judicial observations that carry no compliance obligation, cases where the government is petitioner versus respondent producing structurally different action requirements, coordinate bench and division bench classification affecting the weight and appellate path of the order, and multi-case consolidated judgments where directives apply selectively to individual case numbers within the batch. These are the exact failure modes that produce missed compliance deadlines and time-barred appeals in manual processing workflows.
WHAT CHANGES WHEN VERDICTIQ WORKS: A judgment that would take a legal officer two to three hours to read, interpret, and convert into a department communication is processed into a verified, structured action plan in under 20 minutes. Every action item is cited to a specific page, a specific directive, and a specific extracted value - no interpretation calls buried in a noting file. Zero critical orders are missed because they appeared in paragraph 47 of a 60-page judgment that an overloaded officer skimmed. A dashboard that decision-makers can trust because every item on it has been explicitly verified by a human reviewer before it arrived. Deployable across every department using CCMS at the Karnataka High Court first, then scalable across state government legal cells and eventually across central government ministries tracking litigation at High Courts and the Supreme Court.
